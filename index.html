Projects

Projects

Below are sample datasets from various projects that highlight my expertise in mapping, modeling, and risk assessment. These projects demonstrate the application of advanced techniques and tools, including satellite remote sensing, GIS, and hydrological modeling.

GLOF Mapping and Risk Assessment using Combined Approaches of Satellite Remote Sensing, GIS and Dam Break Modelling

This research focuses on developing a comprehensive methodology for mapping and assessing the risks associated with Glacial Lake Outburst Floods (GLOFs). The study integrates the advanced technologies of satellite remote sensing, Geographic Information Systems (GIS), and dam break modeling to identify potential GLOF hazards and evaluate their impact.

Reservoir sedimentation Assessment using Remote Sensing ,GIS and Hydrological Modelling.

This project aims to evaluate the extent and impact of sedimentation in reservoirs by employing a combination of remote sensing, Geographic Information Systems (GIS), and hydrological modeling techniques. The study addresses the critical issue of sediment accumulation, which affects reservoir capacity, water quality, and the overall efficiency of water resource management systems.

Land Surface Temperature in the Metropolitan Twin Cities Islamabad and Rawalpindi, Pakistan

This project investigates the (LST) variations in the metropolitan twin cities of Islamabad and Rawalpindi, Pakistan. By analyzing LST, the study aims to understand the urban heat island effect and its implications for urban planning and management in these rapidly developing areas and the correlation between LST and land use changes.

Development of an Interactive GIS-Based Web Map for Real Estate Visualization and Analysis

This project focuses on developing an interactive GIS-based web map for real estate, enabling users to visualize properties, analyze zoning, land use, and infrastructure proximity. By integrating real-time data and spatial analysis tools, the platform will enhance decision-making for buyers, sellers, and investors. The map will offer features like filtering, measurement tools, and demographic insights, revolutionizing real estate exploration.

Assessment of (LULC) and (LST) Dynamics as a Result of the (CPEC) Development Using Geospatial Technologies

This project analyzes (LULC) and (LST) changes in District Abbottabad from 2013 to 2022 using geospatial technology. It examines the impacts of urbanization, agriculture, and reforestation on land use and temperature. Satellite imagery and GIS techniques will be used for LULC classification, with thermal data assessing LST variations for sustainable land management insights.

ASSESSMENT OF CROP WATER REQUIREMENT FOR VARIOUS CROPS IN GILGIT, PAKISTAN USING CROPWAT MODEL

This project assesses the crop water requirements for various crops in Gilgit, using the CROPWAT model. Data on climate, crops, and soil will be gathered from FAO CLIMWAT and local sources. The model will estimate (ET₀) and irrigation needs for crops like wheat and maize. The results will help optimize irrigation practices and improve water management in the region.


Glacier & Snow Cover change Assessment and Mapping using Remote Sensing & GIS

Snow cover is vital to climate and water resources, making its monitoring essential. Remote Sensing and GIS provide powerful tools to assess and map snow cover changes over time. Satellite imagery and techniques like the Normalized Difference Snow Index (NDSI) allow for precise detection of snow-covered areas. GIS aids in analyzing these patterns in relation to topography and climate.

Landslide Susceptibility Mapping using GIS, Remote Sensing, and AHP Techniques

This project focuses on mapping landslide susceptibility by integrating the capabilities of Geographic Information Systems (GIS) and remote sensing. Landslide susceptibility mapping is crucial for disaster risk reduction, land use planning, and infrastructure development, particularly in mountainous and hilly regions.A case in District Chitral, KPK, where the community is facing landslides.
